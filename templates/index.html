Air Quality

Think of the AQI as a yardstick that runs from 0 to 500. The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern. For example, an AQI value of 50 or below represents good air quality, while an AQI value over 300 represents hazardous air quality.

Our Services

We provide innovative and data-driven solutions to monitor, predict, and mitigate toxic air pollutants from emerging industrial sources. Our services are designed to enhance urban air quality, support policy-making, and protect public health through advanced AI and real-time visualization tools

Consulting

We offer expert consultation for urban planners, environmental agencies, and industries, helping them understand air pollution patterns and implement sustainable emission control strategies.

Strategy
